The Science Achievers Program is a free program offered to teens 14 and older by the Museum of Science and Industry (MSI). It is program done throught the school year and it has 3 different seessions. The fall session, winter session and spring session, most Science Achievers will go to all three sesssions but some opt out of a session do to a sport, tutoring or any other reason.

To become a Science Achiever you first have to go through the Science Minors program. You can apply to be Science Minor here or on the MSI's website. The Science Minors program is similar to Science Achivers but there are differences. Both Minors and Achivers take place every Saturday for 10 weeks. Minors takes place in the morining for about 3 hours from 9am to noon. Achievers takes place all day from 9am to 4pm with an hour lunch break at noon.

At the end of every Minor session there is a family day. The museum provides food and the Minors will showcase what they learned to family and friends. Achievers on the other hand have a Case Celebration at the end of the year in the spring. Here we showcase what we learned throughout the year and celebrate the Senior graduating. The Seniors are given a gift and in recent years it has usually been a laptop.
